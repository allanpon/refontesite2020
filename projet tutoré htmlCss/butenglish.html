  • Moving on to BUT

A reform of the professional license has been initiated by the Ministry of Higher Education, Research and Innovation. Published in the OJ (official gazette) in December 2019, this reform creates a new professional license that will be delivered in IUTs (university institutes of technology). Called Bachelor of Technology (BUT), this new degree will confer a level bac + 3 to students in IUTs.

Contours of the BUT

From the start of the 2021 academic year, the IUTs will have to offer a 3-year training program, giving students 180 ECTS credits. However, the creation of this new diploma does not do away with the DUT. The students of this new professional license course will also be able to be sanctioned at the intermediate level of 120 credits (level bac + 2) with the delivery of the DUT.

This new 3-year course allows students to obtain a bachelor's degree with the possibility of reorienting themselves from the second year thanks to the set of bridges provided for in the decree which reminds that "the establishment ensures the flexibility of the courses and (...) the reorientation of the students by the organization of bridges between courses".

In the first year of BUT, at least 50% of technological bachelors should be accepted. Existing specialties already existing under the OTC should be retained. The decree provides for the monitoring of 2,000 hours of supervised teaching for specialties in the "production" sector and 1,800 hours for specialties in the "services" sector. A place would be made for work in project mode and students would follow 600 hours of tutored project work and 22 to 26 weeks of internship.

The university bachelor of technology will be based, for two thirds of the volume of hours, on a national program and for one third on local adaptations allowing to take into account the local environment and the reality of the professional world.

Towards the end of the DUT?

The assembly of IUT directors, meeting in September 2019, issued a favorable opinion on the evolution of training in IUTs with the creation of a professional bachelor's degree course called university bachelor of technology. But this reform is also worrying and a forum collected more than 2,600 signatures, including IUT staff.

One of the first concerns is the possible disappearance of the DUT. Officially, the diploma will not disappear since an intermediate qualification, after 2 years, will still be possible. On the other hand, some IUT professionals fear a progressive disappearance of this diploma. The DUT could, little by little, be forgotten, not only by students who would not ask for the intermediate certification, but also by recruiters. However, today this diploma enjoys great recognition in the professional world.

The last sensitive point of the reform: will the level of the GOAL be lower than that of the DUT? Currently, technological baccalaureate holders represent 32% of first-year DUT students, compared to 63% of general baccalaureate holders. The reform intends to impose 50% of technological baccalaureate holders in the BUT to give these profiles a better chance to integrate the program.
